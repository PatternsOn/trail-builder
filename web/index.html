The calculator is designed for pump track builders, BMX/MTB riders, landscape designers and trail planners who need a fast and reliable way to size rollers for smooth flow and speed control.

How to Build a Pump Track Roller

A pump track roller is a smooth mound of soil shaped to help riders generate speed without pedaling. To build a roller, start by shaping a low and round mound with a gradual approach and exit. The typical height is 0.4–1.0 meters depending on rider skill and track speed.

A good roller has a smooth transition, no sharp edges, and a consistent radius. Beginners should aim for longer rollers with gentle slopes, while advanced tracks can use steeper rollers with shorter spacing. The calculator on this page helps estimate roller height, radius, and spacing for predictable and flowy designs.
